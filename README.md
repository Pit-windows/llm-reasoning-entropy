# Analysis of Reasoning in Large Language Models via Conditional Entropy

This repository contains the complete codebase and experimental data used for my Bachelor's thesis at **Università degli Studi di Cassino e del Lazio Meridionale**. The study investigates whether conditional entropy, computed on the final answer of reasoning traces generated by LLMs, can serve as a proxy to quantify the utility of individual reasoning steps, specifically seeking a signal that correlates entropy reduction with correctness.

The full text of the thesis, providing theoretical foundations and a detailed discussion of the results, is available in the root of this repository:
**[Tesi Triennale - Pietro D'Agosto.pdf](./Tesi%20Triennale%20-%20Pietro%20D'Agosto.pdf)**

The implemented pipeline is explained in a top-down approach as follows:

### 1. Overview
The full pipeline manages the workflow from problem selection in the **MATH dataset** to the generation of processed entropy trajectories, which form the basis of the thesis interpretations.

![Main Pipeline](assets/pipeline.svg)

### 2. Traces Generation
Reasoning traces are generated using **Qwen2.5-32B** via Zero-Shot Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting. Traces are then parsed using Regular Expressions (Regex) and stored as structured JSON files to isolate steps ($Z_k$) and final answers ($Y$).

![Traces Generation](assets/traces_gen.svg)

### 3. Entropy Trajectories Computing
Traces are converted into entropy curves relative to a chosen final answer $Y_i$. This allows for the observation of how uncertainty changes as reasoning unfolds.

![Entropy curves computing](assets/entropy_curves.svg)

#### 3.1 Conditional Entropy Calculation
Conditional entropy $H(Y_i|C_k)$ is calculated for each reasoning step using **Teacher Forcing** with **DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-8B** as the inspector model.

![Conditional Entropy computation](assets/entropy_compute.svg)

### 4. Experimental Analysis & Plotting
The experimental evaluation is divided into two distinct analyses:

* **Analysis 1 (Cluster Divergence):** Investigates whether the divergence between trace clusters is maximized for the correct answer. It employs **Cubic Spline resampling** to align traces of varying lengths onto a common domain, calculating the **Pointwise Mean and SEM** (Standard Error of the Mean) for statistical comparison.
* **Analysis 2 (Step-level Dynamics):** A descriptive study observing entropy variations ($\Delta H$) induced by individual steps. It identifies **divergent steps** that act as "scaffolding" (temporarily increasing uncertainty to enable complex logic) and **convergent steps** that refine the solution.
